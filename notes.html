Professionalism

  1. Effective Communication
    • Assertive Communication
      • It requires you to show other person that you understand and appreciate the importance of his feelings.
      • It is to make sure you state the problem clearly without causing the user directly.
      • It is not pushy, bossy, or pushover.
    • Respectful Communication
      • Actively listen
      • Avoid interrupting customer
      • Never condescend
      • Do not argue with customer
      • Maintain positive attitude
      • Project confidence in you
      • Avoid distractions
  2. Getting Answers
    • Ask the right questions
    • Listen to customer's answers
    • Follow with fact-seeking questions
      • Assk open-ended questions
  3. Expectations and Follow-up
    • Timeline
      • set and meet expectations, establish timeline, communicate status with customer
    • Options
      • repair vs replacement: avoid similar problem in the future
      • provide all options and let customer decide
    • Documentation
      • Document service provided
        • Describe the problem
        • Include day and time of work, number of hours
        • Include solution, including parts replaced.
      • Provide proper documentation
    • Follow-up
        • Verify satisfaction by phone call or email.

Safety

  1. EMP
    • Electromagnetic pluse
    • EMP is when electricity improperly jumps from one place to another in ways it can cause damage.
    • e.g. Lighting and nuclear detonations
  2. ESD
    • Electrostatic Discharge
      • ESD is the EMP of tech person's concern
      • ESD means the passage of static electrical charge from one item to another.
      • ESD taek place when two objects that store different amounts (potential) of static electricity come in contact.
      • ESD can destroy computer's sensitive parts.
      • Avoid ESD by grounding yourself to computer device by having you and computer you touch are at same electrical potential.
  3. ESD straps,bags,and mats
  4. Resistors
    • ESD straps and mats use resistors: devices that stop or resist the flow of electricity - to prevent a static charge from racing through the device.
  5. EMI
    • EMI: Electromagnetic interference is when a magnetic field interfering with electronics.
    • EMI is less dangerous than ESD but can still cause serious damage to parts (HDD).
    • Keep magnets away from computer.
  6. RFI
    • RFI is Radio frequency interferenc, strange noises from speakers.
    • RFI becomes big issue when two devices share same frequencies.(phones, WAP, baby monitors, microwave)

Troubleshooting methodology

  1. Identify problem
  2. Establish a theory of probable cause
  3. Test theory to determine the cause
  4. Establish plan of action to resolve the problem and identify potential effects
  5. Implement solution or escalates as necessary
  6. Verify full system functionality and implement preventive measure
  7. Document findings, actions, outcomes, and lessons learned
